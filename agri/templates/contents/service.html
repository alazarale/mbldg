Poultry

Chicken meat and eggs are essential diets of Ethiopians consumed in every household in the country.

They are nutritious, relatively cheaper, and available in rural and urban locations. As the economy of Ethiopia and the population is grow the demand for chicken meat and eggs continue to increase throughout Assela and the surrounding cities. The Government of Ethiopia is encouraging the establishment of both small and commercial scale poultry products as part of its agricultural development plan.

Our goal is to provide poultry products to consumers of Assela and neighboring towns, with the promise of quality and profitable business for investors while also serving as model training center for local farmers. We hope to partner with local universities and international organizations for research and fieldwork opportunities.

We are located in Asella, Wolkesa Kebele in the Oromia Region of Ethiopia

Our poultry farm is housed in a barn built from cinderblocks with a size of 23m x 11.6 m, with 2m taken off the front for feed storage, office and egg storage.

Our chickens are a breed of Bovans Browns, With a maximum density not exceed 8-10 birds per meter square density we could house 2,400 chicks. However, since this is our first trial flock, we have decided to start with 1,500 chicks.

The chicks were ordered through Genesis Farm in Bishoftu (Debre Zeit), a model farm that imports baby chicks from Holland. Bishoftu is 117kms from Assela.

Our supplier of feed is Alema Farm also in Bishoftu while medicine and veterinary services are sourced from Assela and Bishoftu.

Our nests are built with 35cm wide x 35cm deep x 40 cm high. %

We have a thermometer placed evenly down the center of the barn to monitor temperature. The ideal temperature is 21-24 degrees Celsius, and inlets is adjusted to allow for this, particularly with the cooler evenings.

We have two full-time staff (a female and male) working at the barn. These are our poultry farmer workers.

They have received training and continue to be supported with a consultant.

The staff are required a full change out of clothing when entering the service room. Coveralls and boots are provided. When going into the chicken pen, a foot bath is need and staff dip there feet in the disinfect prior to entering the pen. Disinfect and water should is changed daily.

The staff ensure proper management of the barn, and a high level of biosecurity. The entrance way was carefully thought out and allows for two entrances into the barn; one to be used for receiving feed, and the other to be used as the main entrance for employees and to ship the eggs.

BIOSECURITY IS THE MOST IMPORTANT FACTOR IN ORDER TO ENSURE FLOCK HEALTH AND MINIMIZE THE RISK OF EXPOSING THE FLOCK TO DISEASE.

We hope to open Barn number 2 in July 2017 housing 3,000 chickens when the trail period of current flocks end.
